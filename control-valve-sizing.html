Control Valve Sizing (Liquid and Vapor)

Uses the formulas shown in your reference sheet. Units are selectable beside each field.

1) Fluid Properties

Quantity Value Unit Notes
Flow rate – mass, W kg/h preferred for liquid formulas
Pressure upstream, P1 (A)/(G) supported
Pressure downstream, P2 ΔP = P1 − P2
Pressure drop, ΔP (computed) Shown in your chosen unit
Critical flow factor (liquid), Fl (or Cf) — Use ~0.9 if unknown

2) Liquid Flow

Quantity Value Unit Formula / Notes
Density, ρ kg/m³ preferred
Volumetric flow, V (computed) V = W / ρ
Vapor pressure, Pv Absolute
Critical pressure, Pc Absolute
ΔPs (computed) ΔPs = P1 − (0.96 − 0.28 (Pv/Pc)^0.5) Pv
Liquid flow type — Subcritical if ΔP < Fl² ΔPs
Cv (liquid) – subcritical — Cv = 1.6×10^-3 · W / √(ρ · ΔP)
Cv (liquid) – critical — Cv = 1.6×10^-3 · W / (Fl √(ρ · ΔPs))
Cv (liquid) – final — Selected per regime
Selected valve — Binds to Cv lookup table
Selected Cv (auto) — Auto-filled from selection
Valve opening % 100 × Cv(final)/Cv(selected)

3) Vapor / Gas Flow

Quantity Value Unit Formula / Notes
Molecular weight, Mw kg/kmol —
Flowing temperature, T Absolute recommended (K/R)
Specific gravity, Gf (computed) — Gf = Mw/28.96 × 288/T(K)
Compressibility, Z — Default 1.0 if blank
Y factor (computed) — Y = min(1.5, 1.63/Fl √(ΔP/P1))
Cv (vapor) – legacy form — Cv = 0.0545 · W(lb/h) · √(T(°R)·Z/Gf) / (P1(psi) · Y)
Selected valve — Binds to Cv lookup table
Selected Cv (auto) — Auto-filled from selection
Valve opening % 100 × Cv(final)/Cv(selected)

Notes

  1. Liquid Critical Flow means cavitation or flashing.
  2. Butterfly valves are at 90° open.
  3. Default Cf / Fl = 0.9 when unknown.
  4. Check manufacturer’s noise/cavitation limits for final selection.
  5. Cv = US gpm of water at 60 °F with 1 psi differential. Kv = m³/h at 15 °C with 1 bar differential. Kv ≈ 0.856 · Cv.
