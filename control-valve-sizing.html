Control Valve Sizing

Mirrors the supplied reference. Liquids and vapors are sized via Kv in SI; final Cv is reported using Kv = 0.856·Cv (Note 5). Unit selectors sit beside each field. Selected valve comes from the lookup table and auto-fills Selected Cv.

1) Fluid Properties

Quantity Value Unit Notes
Flow Rate – Mass (W) kg/h preferred for liquid Kv
Pressure Upstream (P1) (A)/(G) supported
Pressure Downstream (P2) ΔP = P1 − P2
Pressure Drop (ΔP, computed) Shown in chosen unit
Critical Flow Factor (Fl) — Use ~0.9 if unknown

2) Liquid Flow

Quantity Value Unit Formula / Notes
Density (ρ) kg/m³ preferred
Flow Rate – Volume (V, computed) V = W / ρ (m³/h)
Vapor Pressure (Pv) Absolute
Critical Pressure (Pc) Absolute
Critical Pressure Drop (ΔPs, computed) ΔPs = P1 − (0.96 − 0.28 (Pv/Pc)^0.5) Pv
Type of Liquid Flow — Subcritical if ΔP < Fl² ΔPs
Liquid Subcritical Kv — Kv = V(m³/h)/√(ΔP(bar))
Liquid Critical Kv — Kv = V(m³/h)/(Fl √(ΔPs(bar)))
Liquid Cv – subcritical — Cv = Kv / 0.856 (Note 5)
Liquid Cv – critical — Cv = Kv / 0.856 (Note 5)
Liquid Cv – final — Pick per flow regime
Selected Valve — From Cv lookup table
Selected Cv (auto) — Auto-filled
Valve opening % 100 × Cv(final)/Cv(selected)

3) Vapor / Gas Flow

Quantity Value Unit Formula / Notes
Molecular Weight (Mw) kg/kmol —
Flowing Temperature (T) Absolute recommended (K or R)
Specific Gravity (Gf, computed) — Gf = Mw/28.96 × 288/T(K)
Compressibility (Z) — Default 1.0 if blank
Flow Rate – Vol.Upstream (V1) std vol/day Indicative (ideal gas, upstream)
Flow Rate – Vol.Downstream (V2) std vol/day Indicative (ideal gas, downstream)
Flow Rate – Vol.Standard (Vstd) std vol/day Scaled to 1.01325 bar A
Y factor (computed) — Y = min(1.5, 1.63/Fl √(ΔP/P1))
Gas Kv – subcritical (approx.) — Kv ≈ Qup(m³/h)/√(ΔP(bar))
Gas Kv – critical (approx.) — Kv ≈ Qup(m³/h)/(Fl √(ΔP(bar)))
Gas Cv – final — Cv = Kv/0.856 (Note 5)
Selected Valve — From Cv lookup table
Selected Cv (auto) — Auto-filled
Valve opening % 100 × Cv(final)/Cv(selected)

Notes

  1. Liquid Critical Flow means cavitation or flashing.
  2. Butterfly valves are at 90° open.
  3. Default Cf / Fl = 0.9 when unknown.
  4. Check manufacturer’s noise/cavitation limits for final selection.
  5. Cv is US gpm of water at 60 °F with 1 psi differential. Kv is m³/h of water at 15 °C with 1 bar differential. Relationship: Kv = 0.856 Cv → Cv = Kv/0.856.
