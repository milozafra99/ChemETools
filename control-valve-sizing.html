Control Valve Sizing (Liquid and Vapor)

Based on the calculator shown in the provided reference sheet. Units are selectable beside each field. The tool computes subcritical/choked (critical) status for liquids, Cv requirements, and indicative valve opening given a selected Cv.

1) Fluid Properties

Quantity Value Unit Notes
Flow rate – mass, W Used directly for gas; for liquids converts to V = W/ρ
Pressure upstream, P1 Supports (A)/(G) – absolute/gauge
Pressure downstream, P2 ΔP = P1 − P2 handled in consistent basis
Pressure drop, ΔP Computed
Critical flow factor (liquid), Fl (or Cf) — Typical default ~0.9

2) Liquid Flow

Quantity Value Unit Formula / Notes
Density, ρ kg/m³ (or other)
Volumetric flow, V V = W / ρ
Vapor pressure, Pv Absolute at flowing T
Critical pressure, Pc Used in ΔPs
ΔPs (critical threshold) ΔPs = P1 − (0.96 − 0.28·(Pv/Pc)0.5)·Pv
Liquid flow type — Subcritical if ΔP < Fl² ΔPs; else Critical
Cv (liquid) – subcritical — Cv = 1.6×10−3 · W / √(ρ·ΔP) (legacy metric form)
Cv (liquid) – critical — Cv = 1.6×10−3 · W / (Fl √(ρ·ΔPs))
Cv (liquid) – final — Pick subcritical or critical as determined
Selected valve (name/size) — Optional label
Selected Cv — From Cv lookup or spec
Valve opening % Opening ≈ Cv(final)/Cv(selected) · 100%

3) Vapor / Gas Flow

Quantity Value Unit Formula / Notes
Molecular weight, Mw kg/kmol Hydrocarbon gas ~16–44
Flowing temperature, T Absolute recommended (K or R)
Specific gravity, Gf — Gf = Mw/28.96 · 288/T (legacy variant) or Mw/28.96 at 288 K
Compressibility, Z — Default 0.9–1.0 if unknown
Flow rate – upstream basis, V1 std vol/day V1 = W · R · T · Z / (Mw · P1) with consistent units
Flow rate – downstream basis, V2 std vol/day V2 = W · R · T · Z / (Mw · P2)
Standardized flow, Vstd std vol/day At 20 °C, P = 1.01325 bar A (indicative)
Y factor (pressure ratio) — Y = min(1.5, 1.63 / Fl · √(ΔP/P1))
Cv (vapor) – legacy form — Cv ≈ 0.0545 · W · √(T·Z/Gf) / (P1 · Y), with W in lb/h, T in °R, P1 in psi
Selected valve (name/size) — Optional label
Selected Cv — From Cv lookup or spec
Valve opening % Opening ≈ Cv(final)/Cv(selected) · 100%

Notes

  1. Liquid Critical Flow means cavitation or flashing.
  2. Butterfly valves are at 90° open.
  3. SSW default value Cf (or Fl) = 0.9.
  4. Both wafer and nozzle type check valves are non‑slam, but wafer is cheaper and less robust. The nozzle type is used where catastrophic failure would damage compressors or other critical equipment.
  5. Coefficient Cv signifies the flow [US gal/min] of water at 60 °F through an open valve at 1 psi differential. Coefficient Kv signifies the flow [m³/h] of water at 15 °C through an open valve at 1 bar differential. Kv ≈ 0.856 · Cv.
